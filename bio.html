  • Bio

I'm a '14 John S. Knight fellow at Stanford University. I'm researching on how to build a new narrative for health news and medical information dissemination through the collection, analysis and visualization of data.

For the last five years, I've been pursuing an interdisciplinary path involving healthcare biotech, public health research, and data and social startups, as a member of the launching team of mapping platform CartoDB and co-founder of non-profit porCausa.

I'm a lecturer on digital and data journalism at several universities, including the Knight Center for Journalism in the Americas at the University of Texas in Austin, IE Business School (Madrid), and Universidad de La Sabana (Colombia). I'm also involved in public health research as a member of the multidisciplinary research group on social epidemiology from Universidad de Alcalá (Spain).

Between 1998-2008, I worked at Spanish newspaper El Mundo. I was part of the launching team of elmundo.es, led the 24/7 expansion of the online world affairs coverage, and managed the definition and daily programming of the health channel —the largest health news website in Spanish-language.
